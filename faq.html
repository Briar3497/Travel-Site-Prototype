About Tahini

Island Information

Tucked away in the Pacific Ocean, Taniti is a captivating island getaway that blends adventure, natural beauty, and laid back charm. Though the island spans less than 500 square miles, its landscapes are wonderfully diverse. Visitors are greeted by a mix of soft, sandy beaches and dramatic rocky shores, a calm, scenic harbor, lush tropical rainforests, and striking mountain views that create an unforgettable backdrop for exploration and relaxation. Home to an indigenous population of about 20,000, Taniti has recently emerged as a rising destination as tourism continues to grow alongside its long standing fishing and agricultural traditions.

Taniti offers everything travelers need for a comfortable and enjoyable stay. The island features two supermarkets, two smaller grocery stores, and a 24 hour convenience store, making it easy to stock up at any time. While many visitors come for the beaches, rainforest adventures, and the chance to see the island's active volcano, there's no shortage of additional entertainment. Guests can explore a local history museum, enjoy chartered fishing tours, snorkel in crystal clear waters, zipline through the rainforest canopy, or take in island life at local pubs and a microbrewery. Evenings can be spent dancing at a new club, catching a movie, playing arcade games, bowling, or browsing art galleries. For a truly memorable experience, visitors can also book helicopter rides for breathtaking aerial views of the island.

Most tourists spend their time in Taniti City, known for its native architecture and beautiful white sand beaches surrounding Yellow Leaf Bay. From here, travelers can easily join boat or bus tours of the island, set out on guided rainforest hikes, or visit the volcano for a closer look at Taniti's dramatic natural features.

Getting around the island is simple and convenient. Public buses run throughout Taniti City from 5 a.m. to 11 p.m., seven days a week, while private buses serve other areas of the island. Taxis are readily available in the city, and rental cars can be picked up near the airport. For those who prefer a slower pace, bicycles and helmets are available to rent from several vendors, with helmets required by law. Taniti City itself is flat, walkable, and especially pedestrian-friendly. Many visitors choose to stay near Merriton Landing, an area that's easy to explore on foot and perfectly situated to enjoy the best of the island.

FAQ

What power outlets are used on Taniti?

Power outlets are 120 volts (the same as in the United States).

When can alcohol be served?

Alcohol is not allowed to be served or sold between the hours of midnight and 9:00 a.m.

What is the drinking age?

The drinking age on Taniti is 18, but is not strictly enforced.

Is English spoken in Taniti?

Many younger Tanitians speak fluent English. Very little English is spoken in rural areas, especially by the older residents.

Are there any Medical Centers in Taniti?

There is one hospital and several clinics. The hospital has many multilingual employees.

What is the Crime Rates like on Taniti?

Violent crime is very rare on Taniti, but as tourism increases, there are more reports of pickpocketing and other petty crimes.

Does Taniti have many National Holidays?

Taniti enjoys a large number of national holidays, and many tourist attractions and restaurants will be closed on holidays, so visitors should plan accordingly.

What currency is used?

Taniti uses the U.S. dollar as its currency, but many businesses will also accept euros and yen. Several banks facilitate currency exchange, and many businesses accept major credit cards.
